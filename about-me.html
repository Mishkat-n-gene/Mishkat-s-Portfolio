About Me

My journey in bioinformatics and computational biology

Who I Am

I'm Mishkat Noor, a BS Bioinformatics student at the National University of Science and Technology (NUST), currently in my final year of studies. My work is driven by the synergy between computational science and biology, with a specific focus on developing pragmatic solutions for the medical field.

My Passion

I'm particularly interested in advancing the scope of neuro-bioinformatics by leveraging artificial intelligence and machine learning to interpret complex biological data. Beyond my technical pursuits, I'm committed to refining my scientific communication through rigorous research and peer-reviewed publications, ensuring that my approach to bioinformatics remains both creative and methodologically sound.

My Background

With a strong foundation in molecular biology, genetics, and computational methods, I've developed expertise in:

  • Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) and long-read genomics
  • High-Performance Computing and workflow orchestration (Nextflow, SLURM)
  • Variant calling and genome assembly pipelines
  • AI and Machine Learning applications in bioinformatics
  • Computational epidemiology and pandemic forecasting
  • Molecular dynamics and structural bioinformatics

Beyond Academia

When I'm not working on bioinformatics projects, I'm passionate about storytelling, visual arts (charcoals and canvas), embroidery, and filmmaking. These creative pursuits complement my scientific work and help me develop unique perspectives on problem-solving. I actively participate in leadership roles at NUST, including my work as Deputy Director of Social Media Management for NUST Social Values Society and Director of Publications for the NUST Quiz Club.
